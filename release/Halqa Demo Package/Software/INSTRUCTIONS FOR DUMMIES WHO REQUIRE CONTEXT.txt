PART 1 — WHAT HALQA IS (plain English)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A committee (ROSCA / ballot committee / BC) is a Pakistani savings tradition:
N people each pay a fixed amount every period; each period one member takes the
pot; rotate until everyone has received once = one CYCLE. Zero interest, pure
rotating savings. Huge in Pakistan, mostly run on paper/WhatsApp, plagued by
fraud (organizer absconds) and default (someone takes an early pot then stops).

Halqa keeps that and adds THE POOL-IN-INVESTMENT SYSTEM: the HOST diverts a set
percentage (r, <=25%) of each period's pot into a low-risk investment instead of
handing the whole pot to the turn-holder. The turn-holder gets the rest now; the
reinvested slice earns yield and is paid out or rolled forward per the host's
locked distribution mode.

Differentiators vs every other committee app:
  1. Idle committee money EARNS yield instead of sitting dead.
  2. A credit score + solvency system that attacks the #1 fear: default/fraud.
  3. A turn marketplace with real price discovery (time value of money).
  4. An in-app investment terminal surfacing real Pakistani low-risk schemes.

PART 4 — THE MONEY MODEL (exact math — the heart of the product)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Symbols: N members; C contribution/period (paisa); r reinvest ratio (0..0.25);
G pool/period = N*C; i annualized scheme rate (indicative, NOT guaranteed).

4.1 PER ROUND t (t=1..N)
  Everyone pays C -> gross pool G = N*C into the (simulated) escrow ledger.
  Reinvest slice: I_t = round(r * G)
  Turn-holder payout: Pay_t = G - I_t = (1-r)*N*C
  I_t deployed into the committee's Scheme on the round's payout date.

4.2 OVER ONE CYCLE
  Total invested principal P_inv = Σ I_t = r*N^2*C
  Each member's principal share = r*N*C (equals what they "held back" vs a
  traditional committee). Books balance: paid N*C, received (1-r)*N*C, gap
  r*N*C sits in the investment.

4.3 PROFIT (simple-interest approx; use real scheme accrual once live)
  For slice I_t deployed d_t, liquidated L: profit_t = I_t * i * ((L-d_t)/365)
  Gross cycle profit Π = Σ profit_t
  DEV fee = round(0.05 * Π);  Net to members Π_net = Π - DEV fee
  Per member (equal, clean) = Π_net / N. If someone was late/defaulted, weight by
  paidPrincipalPaisa actually contributed, not headcount.

4.4 WORKED EXAMPLE (ship this as an in-app explainer)
  N=10, C=10,000 PKR, monthly, r=0.20, i=18%/yr, liquidate end of month 11.
  G=100,000; I_t=20,000; Pay_t=80,000. P_inv=200,000; each member principal=20,000.
  Σ months held across slices = 55 -> Π ≈ 20,000*0.18*(55/12) ≈ 16,500.
  DEV fee=825; Π_net≈15,675; per member ≈ 1,567.
  HONEST FRAMING for UI: each member gave up liquidity on 20,000 for the cycle to
  earn ~1,567 (~7.8% on the held-back slice). Modest, real, NOT guaranteed. Early
  turns are still worth more (cash sooner) — that gap is what the marketplace prices.

4.5 DISTRIBUTION (host-locked, NO member vote — per D6)
  SHARE:    at cycle end, pay each member principal + their Π_net share.
  COMPOUND: roll principal + Π_net into the next cycle's investment (committee
            runs another cycle; members may exit at the cycle boundary — PART 6).
  TURN-CASHOUT (host toggle): if enabled, a member may take THEIR accrued profit
            share at their own turn instead of waiting for distribution.

4.6 TURN SALE (marketplace, PART 11)
  Premium X, 0 <= X <= 0.50*Pay_p. DEV keeps 10% of X; seller nets 90%. X=0 => DEV=0.

4.7 DEV REVENUE (Kayani Solutions): 5% of realized cycle profit + 10% of turn-sale
  premiums. NOTE: charging these makes you a financial intermediary -> tax +
  licensing consequences (PART 19). In Stage 1 (no custody) these are RECORDED as
  receivables in the ledger, not actually collected, until a partner enables real flows.

PART 5 — HOST CONFIGURATION (set at creation, locked at start)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
HOST ELIGIBILITY: score >= 700 AND active hosted committees < 5. No other limits

CADENCE (host picks preset + exact number; store resolved periodDays):
  VERY_SHORT 7–14 d | SHORT 30–60 d | MID 90–180 d | LONG 240–365 d.
  Enforce the numeric range per preset. Longer period = more yield runway but
  longer wait + higher default risk (surface tradeoff in the create wizard).

SIZE + JOIN:
  memberCap N: [3, 30] (system safety bounds; member count is ALWAYS its own
    manual field, never derived from cadence).
  joinPolicy: OPEN_UNTIL_FULL | OPEN_UNTIL_DATE | INVITE_ONLY.
  joinDeadline?: after it, no new joins even if not full.
  minMembersToStart: [3, N]. autoStart: bool.

REINVESTMENT: reinvestRatio r in [0, 0.25] (D5). schemeId from curated low-risk
  catalog only. distributionMode SHARE|COMPOUND (locked). allowTurnCashout bool.
  allowTurnSale bool.

PAYMENT SCHEDULE (immutable after start — backbone of accurate credit):
  Host sets per-round contribution DUE DATES (system offers sensible defaults).
  RULE: a member must have every prior due paid AND the current-round due paid at
  least 7 DAYS before their own payout date. After START the whole schedule LOCKS;
  neither host nor member can edit it. Changes require a DEV-approved
  ScheduleChangeRequest (audited). Dates cannot move to hide lateness.

TURN ORDER (locked at start): RANDOM_BALLOT (default, seeded+logged) |
  HOST_ASSIGNED | CREDIT_WEIGHTED (higher score = earlier eligibility; the core
  anti-default lever — D4).

OTHER HOST POWERS: invite (code/link/phone); approve joins (INVITE_ONLY); remove
  member only while FORMING; nudge unpaid member; deploy/liquidate investment
  (host only); pin chat announcements; view payment matrix; request schedule
  change from DEV.
HOST CANNOT: touch member funds directly, edit locked dates, see bank
  credentials, exceed 5 active committees, or force a member to stay past a clean
  cycle exit.

PART 6 — MEMBERSHIP LIFECYCLE: JOIN, LEAVE, BAN (per D6)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
MembershipStatus: ACTIVE | EXITED | BANNED | REMOVED.

JOIN: via invite code/link (respect joinPolicy, joinDeadline, cap, JOIN gate in
  PART 7, and solvency in PART 8). Assigns a turnPosition (per orderMode).

LEAVE (allowed ONLY when clean):
  A member may exit if AND ONLY IF:
    (a) they have zero outstanding dues, AND
    (b) at least one FULL CYCLE has completed (they have received their turn or
        formally waived it), AND
    (c) the committee is at a cycle boundary (for COMPOUND multi-cycle groups) or
        COMPLETED (for single-cycle groups).
  On grant: status EXITED, no penalty, security deposit refunded, +credit for a
  clean completion. A single-cycle committee simply COMPLETES and everyone is
  free — no explicit "leave" needed.

BAN (abandonment before a clean cycle):
  Triggers: member stops paying past grace with no valid dispute; or attempts to
  quit mid-cycle; or defaults after receiving a payout.
  Effects: status BANNED; credit -150 + DEFAULT flag; security deposit FORFEITED
  to the group to cover the shortfall; account cannot join or host new committees
  (rehabilitation path optional/off for MVP). All banned events are audited.
  The group is made whole first from the deposit, then from any accrued
  investment profit share owed to the defaulter, then flagged as a loss (in Stage
  1 the app records the shortfall; real recovery needs a custody partner).

WHY THIS DESIGN: it mirrors real committee discipline (you don't get to walk out
  mid-committee) while giving a fair, clean exit once obligations are met.

PART 7 — CREDIT SCORE ENGINE (accurate, deterministic, idempotent)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Range 300..850. New user = 700. Deterministic point system + hard gates. Every
change writes a CreditEvent (idempotent) + updates User.creditScore. No random
drift, no hidden factors.

EVENT DELTAS (one tunable constants file, utils/credit.ts):
  account_created 0 | solvency_verified +10 (one-time) | installment_on_time +4
  | installment_early(>=3d) +2 (stacks -> +6) | cycle_completed_clean +25
  | turn_sale_completed_clean +5 (both parties) | installment_late_1_3d -10
  | installment_late_4_7d -20 | installment_missed(past grace) -40 + LATE flag
  | default_after_receipt -150 + DEFAULT flag + BAN | dispute_lost -30.
  Clamp to [300,850] after each change.

HARD GATES:
  RECEIVE: must have all prior dues + current-round due paid >=7d before payout,
    else payout skips to next eligible member and you drop toward the end
    (configurable). HOST: score>=700 & <5 active. JOIN: score>=550 (below =
    locked out for MVP). Early positions (1..N/2) require score>=680 when
    orderMode=CREDIT_WEIGHTED.

ACCURACY:
  Late/missed computed against the LOCKED schedule only (unambiguous because
  dates are immutable). A cron job evaluates each round at dueDate, dueDate+grace,
  and payoutDate-7d and writes deltas. Idempotency: unique (userId, roundId,
  checkpoint) so re-runs never double-score. No manual "simulate" button in prod
  (keep any dev-only simulator behind NODE_ENV !== 'production').

PART 8 — ANTI-DEFAULT / SOLVENCY (D4: what's safe + standard in Pakistan)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BLUNT REALITY: you CANNOT read a stranger's bank balance in Pakistan — there is
no open-banking / account-information API for unlicensed third parties, and "Cash
App" doesn't operate here (rails are Raast, JazzCash, Easypaisa, SadaPay,
NayaPay, bank transfer). So a literal "verify they hold 70%" is infeasible now.
We achieve the SAME goal (kill default incentive) with two mechanisms that need
no bank access and match how real committees already manage trust:

  MECHANISM 1 — CREDIT-WEIGHTED TURN ORDER (default, free, ship first)
    Unproven/low-score members get LATER turns. By the time they receive, they've
    already paid most dues, so grab-and-run is impossible. This alone removes most
    default risk.

  MECHANISM 2 — REFUNDABLE SECURITY DEPOSIT for EARLY receivers
    A member receiving an early turn posts a refundable deposit sized to their
    remaining post-payout obligation (suggest 30–70% of remaining dues; later
    turns post less/none). Held in the escrow ledger; refunded on clean cycle;
    FORFEITED to the group on default (PART 6).
    STAGE-1 CAVEAT: with no custody partner yet, the deposit is a RECORDED
    commitment (member transfers on their own rail; host/app confirms receipt).
    It becomes a truly held escrow deposit only in Stage 2. Say "recorded", not
    "secured", in the UI until then.

  OPTIONAL LATER — Guarantor co-sign (a high-score user vouches, -25 if vouchee
    defaults) and, once a partner exists, a real consented balance/income check.

REFRAME OF THE OWNER'S "70% RULE" (enforceable today): "Early receivers must
either be proven payers (good score, later-eligible) OR post a refundable
security deposit covering their remaining dues; and everyone must have all dues
paid >=7 days before their payout." Same protection, actually buildable.

PART 9 — KYC / IDENTITY (D7: max self-serve, no entity deals)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Goal: strongest identity assurance achievable WITHOUT contracting NADRA/banks.
Tiered onboarding:

  LEVEL 0 (account): phone number + OTP (via a self-serve SMS API), email +
    verify link, password (bcrypt, >=8 chars). Device fingerprint stored + bound.
  LEVEL 1 (identity capture): CNIC number (13 digits, format-validated + unique),
    upload CNIC front + back images to a PRIVATE bucket (signed URLs only). Run
    OCR to extract name/number and cross-check the typed CNIC (self-serve OCR API
    or on-device). Reject mismatches.
  LEVEL 2 (biometric): selfie + LIVENESS check + FACE MATCH against the CNIC
    photo (self-serve face API, e.g. AWS Rekognition or an on-device model).
    Store only match score + status, minimize raw biometric retention.
  DUPLICATE DETECTION: one CNIC = one account; block re-registration.

Gating: hosting and receiving early turns require LEVEL 2. Joining low-value
committees may allow LEVEL 1. Set thresholds in config.

HONEST CAVEAT (put in code comments + owner docs): this is strong DETERRENCE, not
legally-verified identity. Only NADRA Verisys confirms a CNIC is real and belongs
to the person. Add Verisys (and a bank-grade check) when a partner exists (D2).
PRIVACY: encrypt documents at rest, private buckets, signed short-lived URLs,
retention policy, and a clear consent screen. Never put CNIC/biometrics in logs
or URLs.

PART 11 — TURN MARKETPLACE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Rules: premium X in [0, 0.5*Pay_p]; DEV keeps 10% of X; seller nets 90%. Buyer
may be a member OR a non-member (non-member must onboard to the required KYC/
credit/solvency level and JOIN as they buy). Guardrails: cannot list a received
turn or with unpaid dues; buyer must clear solvency for the position's remaining
dues; only if committee.allowTurnSale.
Listing shows: committee, position, payout, remaining dues, period, seller score,
premium, and BUYER net cost = remaining dues + X.
On accept (atomic): swap positions; settle premium (DEV 10% recorded); record
buyer obligations; +5 credit to both on clean completion; audit. Settle premium
THROUGH the app ledger; never peer-to-peer outside (fraud + lost fee).

PART 16 — STATE MACHINES (implement exactly)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
COMMITTEE: FORMING --(start)--> ACTIVE --(last payout of final cycle)--> COMPLETED.
  FORMING --(host cancel)--> CANCELLED. Joining-when-full stays FORMING; only
  /start -> ACTIVE (locks schedule+order, creates rounds + round-1 PENDING payments).

ROUND: PENDING --(period opens)--> COLLECTING --(host deploy)--> INVESTED
  --(host liquidate)--> COLLECTING --(payout)--> CLOSED. On payout:
  (a) mark recipient.hasReceived; (b) committee.currentRound++; (c) next round ->
  COLLECTING + create its PENDING payments; (d) if no next round: if COMPOUND and
  members remain, roll into cycleNumber+1 (fresh rounds, allow clean exits at this
  boundary); else committee COMPLETED, +25 credit to clean members, refund
  deposits, notify all.

INVESTMENT: ACTIVE --(liquidate)--> LIQUIDATED (profit realized, DEV 5% taken,
  distributed per host mode). Never pay out with an un-liquidated ACTIVE investment.

PAYMENT: PENDING --(pay on time)--> PAID | --(in grace)--> LATE | --(past grace)-->
  MISSED. Late/Missed drive credit deltas via the idempotent cron scorer;
  repeated/again-post-receipt -> BAN path (PART 6).

PART 17 — SECURITY & ARCHITECTURE PRINCIPLES (senior-dev grade)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  - MONEY IS A LEDGER, NOT A COLUMN. Every paisa movement = an append-only
    LedgerEntry (debit/credit/amount/reason/refs/idempotencyKey). Balances are
    DERIVED (sum of entries), never mutated in place.
  - IDEMPOTENCY EVERYWHERE. Every payment/payout/deploy/liquidate/scoring carries
    an idempotency key; retries are safe.
  - DETERMINISTIC + AUDITED. No randomness in money math; RANDOM_BALLOT uses a
    seeded, logged draw (re-verifiable). AuditLog on every state change.
  - AUTHZ IN TWO PLACES: API is source of truth (role/ownership/membership),
    UI hides controls. Never trust the client.
  - INTEGER PAISA everywhere; round explicitly; test that books balance.
  - SEPARATE SCHEDULER: node-cron/BullMQ worker handles round transitions,
    late-marking, scoring, accrual snapshots. No time logic in request handlers.
  - OBSERVABILITY: structured logs, error tracking (Sentry), admin metrics.
  - TESTS ON MONEY PATHS: unit tests for every PART 4 formula + property test
    "sum in == sum out + fees" over random committees. This is where bugs cost
    real money.
  - SECRETS in env only; rotate; 64-char JWT secret; refresh tokens; rate-limit
    auth. KYC media encrypted, private, signed URLs, never logged.

PART 18 — RISK ANALYSIS (brutally honest: why this dies, why it wins)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY IT COULD FAIL (ranked by lethality):
  F1 REGULATORY (existential). Collecting public money and investing it can be
     deposit-taking (SBP) and/or a collective investment scheme (SECP). Unlicensed
     = shutdown/prosecution risk. Taking profit cuts strengthens the "unlicensed
     intermediary" case. MITIGATED by Stage 1 (no custody) + a licensed partner
     for Stage 2 (PART 19).
  F2 TRUST / SCAM ASSOCIATION. "Committee + someone controls a pooled investment"
     is the exact shape of Pakistani committee scams. A slick app can look MORE
     suspicious. MITIGATED by radical transparency, immutable schedule, audit log,
     strong KYC, host reputation, invite-only launch among real circles.
  F3 CUSTODY. Holding members' float unlicensed ~ unlicensed EMI; host holding it
     ~ a fraud tool. MITIGATED by Stage 1 (money moves on members' own rails, app
     only records) and Stage 2 (licensed custodian holds funds; host only SELECTS
     schemes, partner EXECUTES).
  F4 DEFAULT CASCADE + MARKET LOSS. Early receiver stops paying; or the invested
     slice drops. MITIGATED by credit-weighted order + deposits (PART 8), low-risk
     only + r<=25% (D5), never paying out on an un-liquidated position, and
     showing ranges not promises.
  F5 IMPOSSIBLE PROMISES. Literal bank-balance reads and guaranteed-return graphs
     can't be delivered and invite misleading-promotion claims. MITIGATED by the
     reframed solvency (PART 8) and mandatory "not guaranteed / not advice" copy.
  F6 COLD-START LIQUIDITY. Marketplace needs users. MITIGATED by launching
     member-only, opening to non-members later.
  F7 OPERATIONAL BOTTLENECK. "DEV-only date changes" = manual fiduciary load.
     MITIGATED by immutable-by-design schedules; DEV touches only rare disputes,
     logged.
  F8 TAX/AML. Fees are income; flows may need reporting. MITIGATED by an
     accountant early + AML thresholds + the AuditLog.

WHY IT COULD FLOURISH:
  W1 Massive TAM — committees are everywhere in Pakistan and almost entirely
     analog today; digitizing alone is valuable.
  W2 Real differentiation — yield on idle committee money is genuinely new.
  W3 The trust layer (credit + solvency + immutable schedule + audit + KYC) is
     the moat; it attacks the #1 reason people avoid committees.
  W4 Network effects — hosts import their circles; chat + marketplace add
     stickiness; good hosts build reputation.
  W5 Aligned fees — you earn only when users earn/trade; good optics + incentives.
  W6 Financial-inclusion narrative — pro-saving, pro-yield; attractive to press,
     grants, and eventually regulators IF done compliantly.

GOOD FACTORS: cultural fit, clear value, defensible moat, viral loop.
BAD FACTORS: regulatory heat, scam-shape, custody risk, cold-start, tax/AML load,
infeasible balance-reads (already reframed).
