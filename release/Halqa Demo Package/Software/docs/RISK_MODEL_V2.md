# Halqa Risk Model 2.0

Status: production-oriented Stage-1 record-only model. It measures and discloses risk; it does not imply custody, insurance, guaranteed returns, credit-bureau reporting, or regulated investment execution.

## Decision hierarchy

1. Preserve ledger integrity and integer-paisa accounting.
2. Keep every group policy deterministic, disclosed, versioned and locked at start.
3. Let the host execute the group mandate; require every member to accept the exact policy snapshot.
4. Permit higher allocation only in explicitly labelled HYBRID or INVESTMENT circles.
5. Block extreme-risk schemes and unlicensed rails. External-provider ideas remain guarded adapters, never fake integrations.

## Scores

Each committee receives a 1–10 score and label:

- 1–3 LOW: sovereign, liquid, strong coverage, reliable members.
- 4–6 MEDIUM: moderate market/liquidity/default exposure with adequate controls.
- 7–8 HIGH: material volatility, concentration or locked liquidity. Explicit high-risk consent required.
- 9–10 EXTREME: blocked from deployment in the current product.

The engine combines member reliability (22%), payment behaviour (18%), instrument risk (18%), maturity mismatch (16%), capital allocation (14%), and concentration (12%). Security deposits, payout buffers, liquidity reserves, guarantors and strong member credit reduce—but never erase—the result.

## Quantitative outputs

- committee score and band;
- investment, default, liquidity and concentration sub-scores;
- control-strength score;
- expected-loss reserve in basis points;
- coverage ratio;
- graduated early-turn security deposit requirement;
- downside, expected, upside and stress-loss projections;
- evidence per factor and deterministic recommendations.

## Responsibility model

Members own the decision to participate. Before start, each member must accept a cryptographic hash of the exact policy and model version. Members can inspect allocations, source dates, liquidity, risk factors and all deployments but cannot unilaterally change pooled assets. The host controls execution only within the locked mandate. Policy changes require a new acknowledgement set.

## Enabled controls

- credit-weighted order;
- graduated deposits and dynamic top-ups;
- partial payout buffer and exposure-to-obligation cap;
- profit withholding/offset for unresolved dues;
- fixed progressive late penalties (no compounding interest);
- capital-days profit allocation;
- explainable default-risk scoring;
- smart/peer nudges, countdowns and transparent payment matrix;
- post-receipt default penalty, cooldown and rehabilitation;
- committee-scoped dynamic-price auction marketplace;
- loyalty, education, reliability badges and risk-adjusted leaderboard;
- low/medium/high scheme catalogue with mode-specific risk caps.

## Model governance

Every assessment stores model version, factor evidence, policy hash, score, controls and recommendations. Recalculation creates an immutable history row. Rates are dated indicative inputs and must be refreshed by an administrator from an official source. Model changes require version increments and must never rewrite old assessments.
