# The Problem
Committees (kameti / BC / ROSCA) are how Pakistan actually saves — but they carry two century-old flaws:
- **Fragile:** one member takes their payout and disappears → the group loses months of savings, zero recovery
- **Unrewarding:** the pooled money sits idle between payments, earning nothing while inflation erodes it
- **Invisible:** 20 years of perfect payments builds no credit history and no financial identity

---

# The Habit IS the Market
- **~41% of Pakistanis** have participated in a committee (Oraan research, TechCrunch 2021)
- **~US$5 billion** rotates through committees every year (same source)
- Pakistan is one of just **8 countries holding ~650M of the world's 1.3B unbanked adults** (World Bank Global Findex 2025)
- **~30 percentage point gender gap** in account ownership — among the widest in the world (Findex 2025)
The behaviour already exists at national scale. Nobody needs to be taught to save this way.

---

# The Product: Halqa
The committee people already trust — with four things paper can never do:
- **Protection** — security deposits, payout holdbacks, reliability scores, real consequences for default
- **Profit** — idle pool days and deposits earn on disclosed, Shariah-compliant instruments
- **Reputation** — every on-time payment builds a verified, portable **Credit Passport** a landlord or lender can check
- **Honesty** — halal engines labelled halal; the one conventional option clearly labelled; no guaranteed-return promises, ever

---

# How Members Earn
Two honest sources — and only two:
- **Real yield on idle money:** Islamic money-market, Mudarabah deposit, income-fund and gold-linked sleeves (≈10.8%–15% indicative, dated rates)
- **Disclosed member-to-member mechanics:** an optional early-turn fee (capped at 10%) paid by members who want cash sooner to members who wait
Reference case — 12 members, Rs 140,000 monthly pool: the best-placed member earns **≈ Rs 16,000–17,000/year** on top of her savings. Modelled, disclosed, never guaranteed.

# The Microfinance Opportunity
Microfinance banks spend heavily to find creditworthy borrowers among the undocumented. Halqa generates that data as a by-product:
- Years of **verified, timestamped committee payment history** per member
- The **Credit Passport** becomes an underwriting bridge into a 12.3M-borrower, Rs 604B market
- Committee groups are natural **deposit-mobilisation** channels (Rs 733B sector deposits today)
The committee becomes the credit bureau the sector never had.

# Business Model
**One line: Halqa takes a 5% share of the profit it generates on members' idle money — and 0% of their savings.**
- Classic Mudarabah structure: members provide capital, Halqa does the work, profit is shared
- If members don't earn, Halqa doesn't earn — total alignment
- Revenue scales with **earning balances** (pool float + personal vault), not just user count
- Member-to-member fees (early-turn) pass through 100% — Halqa keeps none
