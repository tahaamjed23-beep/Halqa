Part 1 · The Committee Economy

The instrument

Mechanics (ROSCA — rotating savings and credit association)

  • Fixed group of n members; identical installment each period.
  • One member per period collects the full pool; n periods complete the cycle.
  • Early positions receive interest-free credit, repaid in installments.
  • Late positions hold enforced, disciplined savings.
  • Net transfer over the full cycle is zero; the traded quantity is time.

The dual function

Each member is simultaneously lender and borrower. Members fund each other's turns and accept 0% because each receives a 0% turn in return. No intermediary spread exists — which is why the pricing is sustainable, and why an institution inserting itself must break it.

Foundations

FoundationFunction
Social collateralGroup-embedded enforcement outperforms legal paperwork at this scale (Besley, Coate & Loury, AER 1993).
Riba-free structureNo interest instrument anywhere in the cycle.
Zero overheadNo branches, forms, or minimums; operating cost near zero.
Commitment mechanismThe rigidity is the product: members join in order to be bound to save.

Why banks hold neither side

The credit side runs on social information banks cannot observe; the 0% price requires the absence of a funding spread banks cannot escape. Banks captured only the savings shell (recurring deposit schemes), which loses to the kameti because the rotation is the demanded product.

Part 1 · Failure Modes

How the instrument fails

Case: Sidra Humaid, Karachi, 2022

  • Committees operated across 117 WhatsApp groups.
  • Collections taken into her personal custody.
  • Rs 420 million collected; no written records maintained.
  • No repayment capacity; declared inability to pay.
  • Member recovery: zero. Without records, victims had no legal existence as creditors.

The structural pattern

Self-custody plus absence of records equals unrecoverable loss. The organiser holds both the money and the only memory of who is owed what.

Frequency

  • Large-scale precedents: Double Shah (2005–07); modaraba scandals (2013–14).
  • Small-circle collapses occur continuously and go unreported — a defaulted street committee produces no filing, no record, no statistic.
  • Each unrecorded collapse pushes savers toward smaller, poorer circles; the failure mode taxes the entire system's scale.

The diagnosis

The committee's weakness is not its economics — those have survived a century on every continent. The weakness is the absence of records, collateral, scoring, and recovery.

That absence is the opportunity.

Part 3 · Earning Engines

The composable engine — five families of levers

The profit engine is a set of independent levers grouped into five families. The host switches any combination on at creation; none is mandatory; an unconfigured circle is a plain rotation. Every active lever is disclosed to members before joining.

FamilyLevers and functionStatus
A · Yield
generate the profit pool		Float placement — idle pool placed in an Islamic money-market fund. Deposit remuneration — security deposits earn on the same basis. Group allocation — a host-set slice of the pool invested into a dated scheme.L+B
B · Distribution
split the pool fairly		Capital-days basis — profit split by amount × days committed. Patience weighting — weight rises 1.0×→2.0× by position. Pooled dividend — a round dividend shared evenly rather than to one position.BUILT
C · Behavioural
reward conduct		Early-payment boost — 1.25× weight for consistently early payers. Clean-streak bonus — +5% float profit per consecutive clean round, capped +50%.BUILT
D · Liquidity pricing
price time		Early-access fee — position 1 pays 10% of pool declining to zero; 90% to waiting members, 10% platform.BUILT
E · Compounding
extend the earning period		Delayed distribution — a settle-window before release, lengthening earning time. Profit recycling — realised profit re-committed into the next round's float.BUILT

Families A and E create and extend the pool; B, C and D govern how it is shared and how early liquidity is priced. A previously available prize-draw lever was removed on Shariah grounds.

Early-access fee

Delayed distribution / Profit recycling

Part 4 · Turn Market

Buying and selling turns at a premium BUILT

Mechanics

  • A member lists an upcoming payout position at an asking premium.
  • Constraint: 0 ≤ premium ≤ 50% of the payout — desperation pricing structurally excluded.
  • Bids must exceed the standing floor (highest open bid, else the ask).
  • Settlement is atomic: position swap, premium transfer, and platform fee in one transaction.
  • The buyer pays the seller directly — value remains inside the circle.
  • Platform fee: 10% of the premium.

Function and constraints

  • Prices the committee's time-value openly, replacing informal position-swapping by favour or pressure.
  • Implied cost of acceleration is a fraction of informal lending rates; the counterparty is a fellow member, not a lender.
  • Reliability scores unaffected — trading a position is a preference, not a breach.
  • Group visibility and consent are structural; no trade occurs privately.
  • Labelling rule: circles carrying Shariah-compliant engine labels permit zero-premium swaps only; premium trading operates in configurations that carry no such label.

Part 5 · Vault and Investments

The investment layer BUILT (live deployment LICENCE+BANK)

Two surfaces draw on the same instrument set. The Vault is the member's personal savings and investment account. The Terminal lists the dated-rate schemes into which both committee float and vault balances are placed. All placement is Mudarabah-based; the platform's share is 5% of profit on withdrawal, never principal.

Vault — personal facility

  • Allocation across four sleeves in whole percentages summing to 100.
  • Blended accrual rate = Σ(shareᵢ × rateᵢ); blended risk score shown live.
  • Payout parking, top-ups from Rs 100, full-sweep withdrawal.
  • Growth projection: conservative / expected / optimistic paths, monthly compounding, asymmetric downside on the crypto sleeve.
  • Per-sleeve risk score (/10), volatility range, exit liquidity in days.
  • Money map: states the custody location of every rupee at each stage.

Terminal — scheme catalogue

  • Dated indicative rates with source and as-of date on every scheme.
  • Cadence-to-tenor filtering: only instruments that settle within the circle's period are offered.
  • Committee float and security deposits placed here under the treasury logic (Part 7).
  • The same catalogue backs both the vault sleeves and the circle engines.

Non-custodial position: Halqa never holds units or cash. Escrow is at the bank; units are at the fund trustee; Halqa instructs within member-signed mandates.

Part 7 · Default Protection (3 of 3) — Validation

Agent-based simulation of calendar 2025 BUILT

Method

  • 120,000 agents · 10,441 committees on the real 2025 calendar: Ramadan, both Eids, school-fee seasons, monsoon flooding.
  • Dual-arm, decision-paired randomness: identical agents live the identical year twice — paper arm and platform arm — same incomes, shocks, and temptations. Only the platform differs; outcome differences are causal.
  • Deterministic seed: every figure reproduces exactly on re-run; the simulation executes in continuous integration.

Seasonality

Both arms stress at Eid and peak during the floods — monthly default peak 376 (paper, July) against 173 (platform, August). The platform curve is lower in every month; the widest gap is early-cycle, where credit-weighted ordering concentrates reliable members in exposed turns.
