# Not-selected and guarded strategy register

The supplied source contains 99 numbered proposals, not 100. All 99 are represented in the API strategy catalogue. “Not selected” means intentionally excluded from active execution; it does not mean forgotten.

## Rejected contradictions

- 21 Stablecoin yield farming — custody, FX, counterparty and regulatory exposure are incompatible with the Stage-1 record-only product.
- 22 App-native microloans — turns Halqa into an unlicensed lender and creates correlated default risk.
- 23 Commodity futures — leveraged/variable exposure is unsuitable for scheduled ROSCA liabilities.
- 30 Seasonal opportunity fund — discretionary timing cannot be represented as a deterministic member mandate.
- 42 50% rotating cap — rejected; classic rotating circles remain capped at 25%. Higher allocations require HYBRID/INVESTMENT labels.
- 44 Member-choice reinvestment — rejected because one pooled liability cannot maintain conflicting personal allocations. Responsibility is implemented through informed opt-in/exit before lock.
- 47 Timeliness-weighted profit — rejected in favour of option 48 capital-days, which measures actual contributed capital and time without double-penalising late members.
- 50 Host profit share — rejected; host labour cannot silently dilute member investment ownership.
- 51 Turn-position adjustment — rejected because the auction already prices time value; applying both would double count it.
- 55 Cross-committee turn exchange — rejected because it couples unrelated liabilities and failure domains.
- 57 Individual savings vault — separate regulated custody product, outside Halqa committee scope.
- 58 Referral yield bonus — rejected to avoid pyramid-like economics and undisclosed dilution.
- 71 Daily interest on arrears — rejected in favour of fixed disclosed penalties; avoids compounding/predatory and Shariah concerns.
- 76 Split payout — rejected in favour of a smaller explicit payout buffer plus deposit coverage; preserves the ROSCA turn experience.
- 79 Group joint liability — rejected because innocent members must not receive score penalties for another person's default.
- 90 Credit-bureau reporting — cannot be represented without a signed bureau partnership and lawful reporting basis.
- 92 Shared blacklist — rejected for privacy, due-process and data-quality risk.
- 93 Score decay — rejected because scores should change from attributable behaviour, not passive punishment.
- 97 Blockchain escrow — rejected; it does not solve PKR on/off-ramp, identity, legal custody or recovery.

## Guarded adapters, disabled until prerequisites exist

- 8 Defence Savings Certificates — maturity mismatch/early encashment rules require product-level suitability checks.
- 12–14 Income funds and bank Islamic certificates — require verified current product data and licensed execution partner.
- 18 Gold-linked allocation — enabled only after regulated provider and custody contract.
- 24–26 Corporate Sukuk, term deposits and FX dual pool — require counterparty, eligibility and custody controls.
- 32–34 cross-circle pooling, institutional auction and Murabaha finance — require segregation, legal trust structure and licensed counterparties.
- 35 Premium prize bonds — requires explicit suitability treatment and ownership/custody mechanics.
- 56 Non-member turn purchase — requires full KYC, novation and obligation transfer, not merely a payment.
- 59/99 insurance pools — must be underwritten by a licensed insurer; Halqa cannot claim self-insurance.
- 68 promissory notes — requires enforceable document format, e-signature and lawyer-approved workflow.
- 75 auto-debit, 96 statement matching, 98 payroll deduction — provider/employer adapter seams only until contractual APIs exist.
