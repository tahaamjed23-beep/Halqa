# The problem has a body count
Committees move an estimated Rs 1+ trillion a year between Pakistani households — on trust and memory, with zero records. When it breaks, it breaks big: the Sidra Humaid collapse burned ~Rs 420 million of ordinary savers' money, and her victims had no ledger, no contract, no evidence — nothing to take to a court. 100+ million Pakistanis have committee payment discipline and no credit footprint. The committee is not broken as an idea. It is broken as an institution. Halqa is the institution.

The no-deposit doctrine, proven at scale by Money Fellows in Egypt. Turn order is earned, not given: verified identity and higher reliability rank early; an unproven newcomer is slotted LAST — meaning they pay every installment BEFORE they collect anything. # Fund-the-gap: Halqa's own contribution
A host with 8 of 10 seats filled can press "Start full" — Halqa contributes into the unfilled positions so the circle starts on time. Sponsored slots ALWAYS take the last turns: member money is never exposed to them. Circles using this carry a higher management fee, disclosed before the host confirms. Kills the desperate-stranger admission — the single biggest cause of committee defaults. LIVE (recorded form); real-money version at scale is the LICENCE-stage trigger.

# The turn marketplace
A future payout position is an asset. Members list a turn at a premium; others bid — even from OUTSIDE the circle. Need the money sooner? Buy position 2. Happy to wait? Sell yours and earn the premium. Listing is free; a disclosed service margin on completed trades is platform revenue. Halal-labelled circles allow free swaps only. LIVE.

# Why no licence is needed to operate today
The single question regulators ask: do you hold the customer's money? Halqa does not — money moves member to member; Halqa records, schedules and enforces. That is a software company. The heavy licences are named, understood, and deliberately avoided: SBP EMI (Regulations 2019 — Rs 200 million capital) only if we ever custody funds; SECP NBFC (Investment Finance Services, 2008 Regulations) only if we lend at scale. Both are reached through a bank partner, never bought. Compliance obligations we DO carry: AML Act 2010, PECA 2016, ETO 2002.
