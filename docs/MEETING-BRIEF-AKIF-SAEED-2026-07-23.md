## 4. THE LEGAL STUFF — PG, UNDERTAKING, JAIL & ASSETS *(verified — he'll test it)*

**What you built:**
- **Undertaking (iqrarnama):** a weekly, e-signed promise-to-pay a definite sum.
- **Mutual personal guarantee (PG):** every member guarantees every other member
  (runs member↔member, **never to Halqa** — so Halqa never becomes a lender/creditor).
- **Arbitration clause** for an expedited private route.

**Can a defaulter be sent to jail? — NO. Say this correctly or he'll catch you.**
- In Pakistan you **cannot be imprisoned for a plain civil debt.** A PG/undertaking
  default is **civil**, not criminal. Signing them ≠ "default → prison."
- The **only** criminal/jail route is **§489-F PPC — a *dishonoured cheque* issued
  to repay an obligation** (up to **3 years**). **You dropped the cheque
  mechanism**, so this lever is off the table by design. And even §489-F is
  *punishment only* — the source is explicit: *"recovery cannot be made through
  this process; for recovery there is a civil remedy."*
  [ppc-489-F](https://pakarbiter.com/laws-in-pakistan/pakistan-penal-code-1860/ppc-section-489-f/) ·
  [Courting the Law](https://courtingthelaw.com/2022/06/26/commentary/guest-columnists/section-489-f-ppc-object-scope-trial/)
- The other criminal route, §420 PPC (cheating/fraud), needs *proven deception at
  the outset* — not mere inability to pay.
- **The credible line for him:** *"We don't rely on jail. We rely on making
  unproven members take late turns where they can't default, a fast civil-recovery
  path, and a permanent reputational cost."*

**Can you recover using their assets? — Yes, but only after a court decree.**
- The undertaking (a *written promise for a fixed sum*) and the PG (a *guarantee*)
  both qualify for **Order XXXVII CPC — the summary suit** — which explicitly
  covers *"a debt or liquidated demand arising from a written contract... or a
  guarantee."* The defendant has just **10 days to seek leave to defend**; if none
  is granted, the plaintiff gets a **decree, executable forthwith** → **attachment
  and sale of the defaulter's assets.**
  [Order XXXVII CPC (Pakistan)](https://pakistankanoon.com/statutes/cpc/o-xxxvii/)
- **You cannot** seize assets yourself, or seize "everything" — it needs the
  decree, only up to the decreed amount, and some assets are exempt.
- **Honest timeline:** a summary-suit decree = *months* if uncontested, longer if
  fought; asset attachment follows the decree. Jail = essentially never (no cheque).

**Two honesty flags (don't volunteer, don't deny):** the undertaking/PG texts are
**lawyer-unreviewed drafts** (a banking lawyer must paper them); and since we hold
no collateral, enforcement today = *the signed instruments + the reputational hit +
the fact unproven members can't take early money at all.*

## 5. HOW HALQA WORKS *(if he asks)*

- **Model:** a committee = fixed group (e.g. 10 × Rs 5,000/mo). Each period all
  contribute; one member gets the pot; rotation until all have received once.
- **Halqa's three roles:** the **Ledger** (double-entry record of every rupee), the
  **Treasury** (schedules turns/timelines), the **Credit Bureau** (builds each
  member's reliability record → Credit Passport).
- **Stack:** TypeScript throughout — Node/Express/Prisma/PostgreSQL API, React/Vite
  web, Vercel + Supabase. Live prototype: halqa-seven.vercel.app.
- **Flow:** signup (phone-OTP + CNIC + app PIN) → join a circle & pick a turn *your
  tenure allows* → sign the mutual PG + undertaking → transfers recorded on due
  dates → payout to the turn-holder → every event scored on the ledger.

## 11. LICENSING — WHAT LETS US OPERATE, VERIFIED *(with links)*

**What we need to be legitimate (minimum, and we don't have it yet):**
- **Register a company at SECP** via the eServices portal (name reservation → MOA/
  AOA → incorporation certificate); the system **auto-integrates with FBR for the
  NTN** (National Tax Number). This is step one — a private limited company + NTN.
  [SECP eServices](https://eservices.secp.gov.pk/) ·
  [FBR IRIS](https://iris.fbr.gov.pk/) ·
  [PK fintech regulation overview (Lexology)](https://www.lexology.com/library/detail.aspx?g=84bbef62-a654-45f6-abd8-d0b64ef5718a)

**What we do NOT need — *as long as we stay record-only* (this is the whole argument):**
- **EMI licence (SBP)** — required only to **issue or hold electronic money / accept
  funds from the public.** We do neither (money moves member-to-member; we record).
  [SBP EMI Regulations 2019](https://www.sbp.org.pk/psd/2019/C1-Annex-A.pdf) ·
  [Revised 2023](https://www.sbp.org.pk/press/2023/Pr-21-Jun-2023-1.pdf)
- **NBFC lending licence (SECP)** — required only to **lend.** We don't lend.
- **NBFC "Investment Finance Services" licence (SECP)** — required to **pool and
  invest members' funds** (T-bills/Sukuk/funds). This is *exactly* the trigger for
  the investment layer — which is why it stays a licensed-future product.
  [SECP Investment Finance Services](https://www.secp.gov.pk/licensing/nbfcs/investment-finance-services/)

**The pathway to pilot legally — the SECP Regulatory Sandbox:**
- SECP runs a **Regulatory Sandbox** in cohorts (1st cohort 2020, 2nd 2021+) — a
  controlled, time-limited live test (usually 6–12 months) for innovative models
  that don't fit existing rules. **Its rules expressly allow applications from
  *unregistered start-ups intending to register if the test succeeds*** — which
  fits Halqa exactly. **This is the single most valuable door Akif can open.**
  [SECP Regulatory Sandbox](https://www.secp.gov.pk/regulatory-sandbox/)
